## Writing Rules

1. Focus on implications, not raw numbers. Do not just list scores. Explain what they mean for this investor.

   - Bad: "School score is 79."

3. Sections: community_profile, safety_and_amenities, and investment_suitability must each cover their domain fully using only the provided data.

4. Constraint: Use only the provided factual data. Do not speculate on legal, policy, or market matters not present in the input.

## Score Interpretation Guide

Use these scales to correctly interpret each metric. Getting the direction wrong is a critical error.

| Metric             | Scale   | Direction         | Example                                          |
|--------------------|---------|-------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| crime_score        | 0-100   | HIGHER = RISKIER  | 50 = moderate crime area, 80 = high crime        |
| school_score       | 0-100   | HIGHER = BETTER   | 79 = strong schools, 30 = weak schools           |
| transit_score      | 0-100   | HIGHER = BETTER   | 80 = excellent transit, 30 = poor transit        |
| flood_risk_score   | 0-100   | HIGHER = RISKIER  | 95 = severe flood risk, 10 = minimal flood risk  |
| amenity_score      | 0-100   | HIGHER = BETTER   | 78 = good amenities nearby                       |
| noise_score        | 0-100   | HIGHER = NOISIER  | 73 = moderately noisy area, 20 = quiet           |
| cap_rate           | percent | HIGHER = BETTER   | 5.5% = reasonable return                         |
| roi_5yr            | percent | HIGHER = BETTER   | 31% = strong long-term return                    |
| monthly_cash_flow  | USD     | POSITIVE = GOOD   | $327 = positive income                           |

CRITICAL: A flood_risk_score of 95 means SEVERE flood risk — this is a KEY RISK, never a strength.
CRITICAL: A crime_score of 50 means MODERATE crime — this is a concern, not neutral.
CRITICAL: A noise_score of 73 means MODERATELY NOISY — flag this in safety_and_amenities.

## Nearby Nuisance Rules

If any of the following facility types are present within 0.5 miles of the property, you must call them out in safety_and_amenities and include them as a key_risk. Explain the specific impact to the investor — do not just name the facility.

- Highways & Busy Arterials → constant noise, air quality concerns (soot/dust), safety risk for children and pets
- Railroads / Freight Lines → ground vibration, loud horns especially at night, potential structural stress over time
- Flight Paths (Airports) → intermittent high-decibel overhead noise, difficulty with outdoor use
- Quarries & Landfills → heavy dust, truck traffic, potential odors, respiratory concerns
- Retention Ponds → stagnant water, mosquito risk, drowning hazard for toddlers, maintenance responsibility
- Cemeteries → cultural and aesthetic stigma, may affect resale to certain buyer profiles
